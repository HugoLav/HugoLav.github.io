Lecture notes

The instructor in charge is Richard Anstee, he posts lecture notes, practice quizzes, and more here. In particular, you can find in this document the theorems about LP and duality that you need to know.

In addition to Anstee's lecture notes, you can find below additional material.

  • How to perform a pivot?.
  • The Two Phase method.
  • Why does the simplex algorithm work?.
  • What does complementary slackness look like in practice?.
  • The revised simplex formulas and their proof.
  • The economic significance of dual variables: an example.
  • Reminders about matrix manipulations.

Day to day info

  • 01/06. Example of the soldiers' diet (more historical information can be found for instance here) and outline of the course. The software LINDO can be downloaded at this adress.
  • 01/08. "Big data" example with some reminder of Linear algebra. The equivalence between the linear and non-linear problems will be treated more carefully next lecture.
  • 01/10. End of the big data example. Definition of LP, LP in standard (inequality) form and how to put a LP in standard inequality form. You will be tested on the latter in quizz 1 (practice quizzes are available on Richard Anstee's website).
  • 01/13. How to put a LP in standard inequality form, continued. Definition of the slack variables and of a dictionary. Example of a pivot to optimality.
  • 01/15. Class is cancelled. Teaching hours are cancelled. You can find in this pdf a detailed example of the pivot's method with practice examples. I also remind you that lecture notes and practice quizzes are availbe on the webpage of the other section.
  • 01/17. Another pivot to optimality and discussion of why optimality is reached. First quiz (about how to put a LP in standard form and the pivoting process), note that there were 4 different versions of the quiz.
  • 01/20. Two Phase Method and first example of degeneracy. We will talk about unbounded and infeasible LPs on Wednesday. See also this flowchart of the Two Phase method with examples.
  • 01/22. Example of an infeasible LP and example of a unbounded LP.
  • 01/24. Discussion about why the simplex method works, see the slides of the lecture. Remarks about the finiteness of the number of dictionaries, as well as degeneracy and cycling. Second quiz about the Two Phase method.
  • 01/27. Motivation for the duality theory following the example of the textbook. Dual of a LP in standard inequality form.
  • 01/29. Weak duality and the consequence for checking optimality. Complementary slackness. The example I did at the end of the class is treated in details in this pdf.
  • 01/31. First assignment due (Solution). End of the example on complementary slackness. Statement of the theorem on strong duality. Revised simplex formulas and their proof, summarized in in this pdf.
  • 02/03. Additional comments on the proof of the revised simplex formulas. Proof of the strong duality theorem.
  • 02/05. Analysis of what happens when the right hand side of the constraints is modified. Economic interpretation of dual variables in a cynical example.
  • 02/07. Theorem of the alternative and its proof. Third quiz about duality.
  • 02/10. Revised simplex method. Choice of the entering and leaving variable, and update of the matrix B with the Eta matrix. See this fully worked out example by Richard Anstee.
  • 02/12. More comments about the update of B with the Eta matrix. Detailed computations on Anstee's example.
